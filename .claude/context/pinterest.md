## Why Pinterest Matters for Posters

- Pinterest has 450M+ monthly active users
- Visual product content (art prints, posters, wall decor) is among the highest-performing content on Pinterest
- Pins have a long half-life — a pin can drive traffic for months or years (unlike Instagram/TikTok)
- Pinterest users are actively in discovery/shopping mode, not passive scrollers
- Pinterest traffic converts well to both Etsy listings and own-site product pages

## Pinning Strategy

**What to pin:**
- Product mockups (poster in a styled room setting)
- Flat lay shots (poster on desk/floor with props)
- Lifestyle context (art on gallery walls, above sofas, etc.)
- Niche-themed boards (e.g., "Minimalist Bedroom Art", "Vintage Botanical Prints", "Boho Wall Decor")

**Where to link pins:**
- High-trust, impulse buys → link to Etsy listing
- Email capture / brand building → link to postergenius.ca product page
- Split test both and track which converts better

**Frequency:** 5–15 pins/day is the Pinterest sweet spot for growth. Batch-schedule using Pinterest's native scheduler or Tailwind.

**Board strategy:**
- Create 10–15 niche boards with keyword-rich titles
- Pin your own products AND curated related content (3:1 ratio of curated to own is fine)
- Board titles and descriptions should use exact search terms buyers use

## Image Source

- Primary: Cloudinary library (already integrated with the site — use product images directly)
- Secondary: generate mockup variants for different room styles/aesthetics

## Metrics to Track

- Monthly impressions (Pinterest analytics)
- Outbound clicks (how many clicks went to Etsy or site)
- Top-performing pins by saves and clicks
- Which boards drive the most traffic
